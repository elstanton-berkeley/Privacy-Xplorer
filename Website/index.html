Privacy Xplorer

Mission Statement...

Our project helps to bring to light the impact of the General Data Project Regulation (GDPR) which came into effect on May 25, 2018. Leveraging our historical view helps to show which companies were leaders in this space and which were laggers.

We focused on 3 important attributes related to GDPR alignment: users right to edit, users right to access, and users right to delete their personal data a digital company may collect on them.

This next thing

Privacy policies are notoriously complicated, confusing, and filled with legal jargon. In today's connected society, we are each leaving a detailed and intricate trail of personal data.

More recently, regulations are being put in to place to protect consumers and increase the responsbilities of companies to share what they are tracking on a given consumer and, importantly, give them options on how that data is being used.

We wanted to address this with a longitudinal analysis to allow users to understand how a company's privacy policy has changed over time.

We leveraged the Princeton-Leuven Longitudinal Corpus of Privacy Policies which included over 1 million snapshots from more than 130,000 websites across two decades.
