Privacy Xplorer's Mission Statement

Privacy Xplorer provides regulators and researchers a platform to quickly view and analyze the impact of data privacy regulations on privacy policies over time.

What is a Privacy Policy?

Privacy Policy: an internal statement that governs an organization or entity's handling practices of personal information.

These policies disclose how a company gathers, uses and manages their customers’ data.

Structure

Privacy policies are made up of smaller sections, called "segments". Each of these segments, within a company’s privacy policy, has a purpose that provides insight on the kinds of rules or regulations the company follows.

Increased Complexity

Over the last twenty years, privacy policies have almost than doubled in length and the legal and technical jargon has become more complex - making it even more difficult for the average person to fully comprehend the content within these policies.

This is due to the increase in data privacy regulations.

What Are Data Privacy Regulations?

Overview

Since the 1970s, regulations have been put in place to help govern the collection, use and distribution of consumers's personal information.

More recently, these regulations have been focused on protecting the consumer by requiring companies to disclose what data they track and how it is used.

General Data Project Regulation (GDPR)

In April 2016, the European Union announced/adopted the General Data Project Regulation (GDPR) that set a new standard on how people can access information about them and placed limits on what organisations can do with personal data. These regulations apply to any organization that does business in the EU, regardless of where the organization is based.

Announced in April 2016, GDPR was not strictly enforced until May 2018, giving impacted organizations time to implement these new rules and update their privacy policies.

GDPR Regulations

For example, under GDPR, organizations are required to:

  • Notify customers of data breaches involving personal data
  • Provide customers access to view the data collected on them
  • Request constomers' consent on how their data is collected and used
  • Grant customers the right to delete their personal data

Privacy Xplorer's Analysis

Privacy Xplorer brings to light the impact of the General Data Project Regulation (GDPR) since its announcement in May 2016.

By leveraging historical snapshots of over 100,000 companies' privacy policies, Privacy Xplorer provides a longitudinal analysis on how a company's data policies have changed over time.

The Focus

We wanted to address this with a longitudinal analysis to allow users to understand how a company's privacy policy has changed over time.

Leveraging our historical view helps to show which companies were leaders in this space and which were laggers.

We focused on 3 important attributes related to GDPR alignment: users right to edit, users right to access, and users right to delete their personal data a digital company may collect on them.

The Data

We leveraged the Princeton-Leuven Longitudinal Corpus of Privacy Policies which included over 1 million snapshots from more than 130,000 websites across two decades.
