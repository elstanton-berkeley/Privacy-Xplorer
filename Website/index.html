What Are Data Privacy Regulations?

Since the 1970s, regulations have been put in place to help govern the collection, use and distribution of consumers's personal information. More recently, these regulations have been focused on protecting the consumer by requiring companies to disclose what data they track and how it is used.



Regulations Timeline

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

Adopted in April 2016 and enforced in May 2018, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a major overhaul of data protection and privacy affecting companies that operate in Europe. GDPR aims to give individuals greater transparency and control over the collection and use of their personal data.

GDPR increased users’ rights over their personal data by requiring that companies provide users with the ability to access, edit and request deletion of their personal data. In addition, GDPR expanded the definition of personal data to include cookies, and as such companies are required to provide users with information on the collection and option to disable cookies.

The Data

Online Privacy Policies

Training Data

The Online Privacy Policies (Opp-115) corpus is a collection of 115 website privacy policies labeled by law students to specify specific data practices within the policy.

Link to Opp-115 Website

Princeton-Leuven

Testing Data

The Princeton-Leuven Longitudinal Corpus of Privacy Policies is a non-labeled dataset of 910,000 privacy policy snapshots from 130,000 websites, spanning over two decades.

Link to Princeton-Leuven Website

Model Architecture

Privacy Xplorer utilizes a two-step, semi-supervised classification model, consisting of six fine-tuned LegalBERT models.

These models were initially trained using the Online Privacy Policies (Opp-115) corpus. However, due to the size of the Opp-115 dataset, semi-supervised techniques were later applied to increase the size of the traing data, which in turned drastically improved the accuracy of the models.

Privacy Xplorer

Privacy Xplorer provides regulators and researchers a platform to quickly view and analyze the impact of recent data privacy regulations on over 130,000 companies' privacy policies over time.

By leveraging modern natural language processing techniques, we were able to classify a user's ability to edit, access or delete their data in over one million privacy policies to create a dataset that is the foundation of our analysis.

Disclaimer

All analysis is presented for information purposes. Privacy Xplorer relied heavily rely on machine learning and while we achieved high accuracy on a variety of tasks, the anlysis may occasionally reflect inaccurate user data rights within a company's privacy policy.
