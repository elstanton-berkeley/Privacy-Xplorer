Preface

Own your privacy

Privacy may be important to you...

But who has the time to read every privacy policy? Donald and Cranor estimated that it would take an average user 201 hours to read all the privacy policies encountered in a year.

And regulations are continuing to evolve...

The General Data Protection Act of Europe went into effect in May 2018 followed by the California Consumer Privacy Act in June 2018.

Not all privacy policies are created equal

We realize that not all companies can be compared side-by-side. A social network will require a different privacy policy than an ecommerce company, for example.

So how can we help?

Privacy XPlorer was built to allow regulators, companies and consumers view changes in privacy policies over time to look for trends as well as industry specific comparisons.

Powerful Visualizations

Through the use of both historical visualizations and side-by-side company comparisons we enable the end user to more intiatuively understand how their data is being used.

Newer datasets

Combining key data sources as well as key new data sources to continue to increment on how privacy policy data is analyzed.

Chapter I

Our Data

"Data will talk to you if you're willing to listen."
-- Jim Bergeson

We leveraged multiple datasets for both display and training purposes.


This included the Princeton-Leuven Longitudinal Corpus of Privacy Policies, a reference dataset of over 1 million privacy policy snapshots from more than 100,000 websites, spanning over two decades.


We also used the OPP-115 Corpus (Online Privacy Policies, set of 115) is a collection of website privacy policies (i.e., in natural language) with annotations that specify data practices in the text. Each privacy policy was read and annotated by three graduate students in law.
